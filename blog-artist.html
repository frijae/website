About

Baby Smoove is an American rapper from Detroit, Michigan, known for his distinctive laid-back delivery, clever wordplay, and luxurious lifestyle lyrics. He began gaining attention in the late 2010s as part of Detroit’s new wave of underground hip-hop, carving out a niche with his relaxed vocal tone, slick punchlines, and high-end fashion references. Influenced by Detroit’s gritty sound but opting for a more mellow, understated flow, Smoove quickly stood out among his peers.

Early Life

Detroit rapper Baby Smoove (Jaelin Parker) grew up on the city's east side, experiencing poverty and a desire for a suburban life, starting music at 15 in a friend's studio and finding his voice through raw, relatable storytelling influenced by real-life struggles and artists like Eminem, developing his signature subdued flow while navigating a quiet childhood and graduating high school despite skipping classes.

First Breakthrough

Baby Smoove’s breakthrough came with the release of his mixtape, “Why so Serious,” which introduced his distinct flow and raw storytelling. This project was a game-changer. It showcased his ability to weave personal experiences into compelling narratives. The mixtape gained traction, creating a buzz that positioned him as a promising artist to watch.
